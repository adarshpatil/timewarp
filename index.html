Brief Bio

I am a PhD student, with an ARM fellowship, at the University of Edinburgh advised by Vijay Nagarajan.
My research interests broadly lie in the area of "Memory Systems Design" - disaggregation, consistency, coherence, RAS features, persistency and virtual memory. My ethos of wholistic memory design is reflected in all my works, each of which span several aspects of my proficiency.

Formerly, I worked at Intel as a Research Scientist (2017-19) and at Goldman Sachs as a Technology Analyst (2012-14).
I received my Masters (by research) degree from Indian Institute of Science working with Prof. R Govindarajan and my Bachelors degree from M. S Ramaiah Inst. of Tech.

Outside programming I am a endurance sports hobbyist: Marathons, Duathlons, Trekking and more.

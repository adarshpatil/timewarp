Brief Bio

I am a PhD student, with an ARM fellowship, at the School of Informatics, University of Edinburgh advised by Prof Vijay Nagarajan.
My research interests broadly lie in the area of Memory Systems Design - DRAM, NVM, RAS features, caches, TLBs.

Formerly I was a Research Scientist at Intel (2017--2019) where I worked on optimizing HPC apps and Neural Net frameworks.
I received my M.Sc Engineering degree from Indian Institute of Science working with Prof. R Govindarajan.
I was a Technology Analyst at Goldman Sachs (2012--2014) in the Virtualization and Linux platform engineering group after receiving my Bachelors degree from M. S Ramaiah Inst. of Tech.

Outside programming I am a endurance sports hobbyist: Marathons, Duathlons, Trekking and more.
